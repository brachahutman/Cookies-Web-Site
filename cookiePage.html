Basic Techniques

  1. Always leave enough time for cookie baking. Check the amount of time the recipe will take you. You don't want underdone cookies!
  2. Cookies are often formed in a ball shape. Roll a small chunk of dough gently in your hands, until it resembles an even ball. For medium-sized cookies, the ball should be about an inch in width. For many cookie types, you can place your ball gently on a cookie sheet, and it will spread into a familiar flat cookie while it's baking. Some recipes require that you flatten the ball into a cookie shape.
  3. The best way to bake cookies is with a cookie sheet. You can purchase one of these long, flat metal pans for a minimal fee, or use a disposable option.
  4. Always spray your cookie sheet with cooking spray or coat with butter before placing cookies. This will prevent cookies from sticking to the pan
  5. You can usually tell when a cookie is done when it turns golden at the edges. Do not leave your cookies in the oven for too long or they will burn!
